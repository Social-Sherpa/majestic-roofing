CONVERSION RULES — FOLLOW ALL WITHOUT EXCEPTION

1\. One page, one goal.

The only actions on this page are: call \[PHONE NUMBER\], or submit a short form. No navigation menu. No external links. No distractions. Every element exists to push the visitor towards one of those two actions.

2\. The hero must convert on its own.

If a visitor sees nothing below the fold, they must still have enough information and trust to act. Headline, subheadline, 3 trust bullets, phone number in large text, and the short form — all visible above the fold on desktop. On mobile: headline, large tap-to-call button, and the form. Nothing else above the fold.

3\. The form has exactly 3–4 fields maximum.

Name, Phone Number, Postcode, and a single dropdown for service type. No long message boxes. No GDPR checkbox walls. The submit button must be action-oriented and specific — never just "Submit." It must reference the key USP or offer, e.g. *"Book My Free \[USP ACTION\] →"*

4\. The phone number must appear at least 5 times across the page — sticky header, hero, mid-page CTA, final CTA, and footer. On mobile it must always be a tap-to-call tel: link. Never plain text only.

5\. Images must be real, British, and contextually accurate.

No American houses. No generic stock photos of workers pointing at clipboards. Use images that show: British \[PROPERTY TYPE — e.g. terraced, semi-detached, Victorian, stone cottage\] homes, tradespeople working in overcast British weather, close-up detail shots of \[SERVICE-RELEVANT DETAILS — e.g. roof tiles, lead flashing, ridge tiles, guttering, fascias\]. Every image must reinforce the message of the section it sits in. Provide a clearly labelled image list at the end with precise descriptions for each image needed.

6\. Social proof must be visible without scrolling on desktop.

Star ratings, review count, and at least one testimonial must appear in the first two sections of the page.

7\. The key USP gets its own dedicated section — not a bullet point.

Explain in plain English what \[KEY USP\] is, why it matters to the homeowner specifically, and exactly what happens when they use it. Include a strong supporting visual.

8\. Every section must end with a micro-CTA.

A phone number, a "Get a Free Quote" button, or both. The reader must never be more than one scroll away from a way to contact.

9\. Objection handling must be woven throughout — not isolated in an FAQ at the bottom.

* Address price anxiety in the hero  
* Address trust in the social proof section  
* Address disruption in the process section  
* Address reliability in the why-choose-us section

10\. The guarantee must be prominent and specific.

Not *"all work guaranteed."* State what is covered, for how long, and what the customer does if something goes wrong. Make it feel like a real commitment.

COPY RULES — STRICTLY ENFORCED

* British English throughout. Correct spellings: colour, neighbour, recognise, kerb, authorised, organisation.

COLOUR & DESIGN RULES — CRITICAL

The brand colour \[PRIMARY BRAND COLOUR\] must be used sparingly.

Study how Prestige Roofers, Aventus Roofing, and Hodgson Roofing use colour. The brand colour appears only on: CTA buttons, the footer background, and one or two key accent elements. The rest of the page uses white, off-white, light grey, and near-black for text. This keeps the page looking professional and clean — not cartoonish.

Specifically:

* Backgrounds: White (\#ffffff), off-white (\#f8f8f8), and light grey (\#f2f2f2) for alternating sections. The brand colour is not used as a section background except for the footer and the final CTA section.  
* Text: Near-black (\#1a1a1a) for headings. Dark grey (\#444444) for body copy. Never white text on a coloured background except in the footer and final CTA.  
* Buttons: Brand colour or action colour background with white or dark text — used for CTAs only.  
* Accents: Thin coloured borders, underlines, or icon colours — used sparingly to draw the eye.  
* The page must look like a premium, professional UK trades business website — not a brightly coloured template.
