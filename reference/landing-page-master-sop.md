# SOP: The Perfect Google Ads Landing Page

> This is the single source of truth for all landing page design, copy, and technical decisions. Read it fully before building or modifying any landing page. Every rule here applies to every client unless the client's own CLAUDE.md explicitly overrides it.

## Purpose

Every landing page we build has one goal: generate inbound enquiries (calls and form submissions) from visitors who have just clicked a Google Ad. Each element on the page must earn its place. If it does not push the visitor towards converting, it does not belong on the page.

---

## Part 1 — Pre-Design: Brand Analysis & Ad Congruency

Before a single element is designed, complete a brand and congruency analysis. A landing page that looks disconnected from the ad or the client's brand will lose trust immediately and increase bounce rate, which directly harms Google Quality Score.

### 1.1 Brand Analysis

Visit the client's main website before designing anything. Extract and document:

- **Primary colour** (hex code) — used sparingly, only on CTA buttons, footer, and key accents
- **Secondary / action colour** (hex code) — the colour used to drive action
- **Typography** — identify the font style (clean sans-serif, bold headings, etc.)
- **Visual style** — professional, local, no-nonsense, premium, etc.
- **Tone of voice** — straight-talking, trustworthy, friendly, etc.

The landing page must feel like a natural extension of the client's existing brand. It must not look like a different business.

### 1.2 Ad-to-Page Congruency

The user's journey from search query to ad click to landing page must be seamless. Any disconnect causes bounces, wastes ad spend, and damages Quality Score.

- The main **H1 headline** must directly mirror the primary keyword and the headline of the ad that was clicked
- Every **offer, USP, or call to action** mentioned in the ad must be prominently displayed above the fold
- Each landing page must be built around a **single primary keyword or tightly grouped keyword theme**
- The user must be able to confirm within **two seconds** of landing that they are in the right place

### 1.3 Colour & Design Restraint

Colour must be used with discipline. The brand colour appears only on CTA buttons, the footer, and one or two key accent elements. The rest of the page uses neutral tones.

- **Section backgrounds:** White (#ffffff), off-white (#f8f8f8), and light grey (#f2f2f2) — alternating sections
- **Text:** Near-black (#1a1a1a) for headings, dark grey (#444444) for body copy
- **Buttons:** Brand or action colour background with white text — CTAs only
- **Accents:** Thin coloured borders, underlines, or icon colours — used sparingly

The page must look like a premium, professional UK trades business — not a brightly coloured template.

## Part 2 — Page Structure: Section by Section

Build the page in the following order. Each section has a specific job. Do not combine sections or skip any.

### Section 1 — Sticky Header

Fixed at the top of the screen as the user scrolls. Must be present at all times.

- **Design:** White or very light background. Thin bottom border only — no heavy coloured bar
- **Logo:** Left-aligned
- **Phone number:** Centre-aligned with a phone icon. Must be a tap-to-call link on mobile
- **CTA button:** Right-aligned. "Free Quote" in the action colour
- The phone number must be the local tracking number assigned to this campaign

### Section 2 — Hero Section (Above the Fold)

The hero section must convert on its own. If a visitor sees nothing below the fold, they must still have enough information and trust to act.

- **Hero image:** Full-width, contextually accurate. British homes and tradespeople in realistic settings. Dark overlay at 60–70% opacity for text readability. No American houses. No generic stock photos
- **Headline (H1):** Left-aligned white copy. Outcome-driven — state a specific benefit or result, never a category label. Must match the ad headline and primary keyword
- **Subheadline:** Names the target location and reinforces the key USP
- **Trust bullets:** Three short bullets with tick icons (e.g. "Fully Insured", "Written Guarantee", "Free No-Obligation Quote")
- **Primary CTA:** Large tap-to-call button, visible and prominent
- **Lead form:** Right side on desktop, stacked below on mobile. White background with subtle shadow. 3–4 fields max (Name, Phone, Postcode, Service Type dropdown). CTA button in action colour with specific copy (e.g. "Get My Free Quote"). Small urgency line beneath (e.g. "We typically respond within 1 hour")
- **Trust signal:** Visible review count with a recognisable platform logo (Google Reviews, Checkatrade) directly in the hero

### Section 3 — Trust Strip

Placed immediately below the hero. A single, clean horizontal bar that reinforces credibility at first glance.

- **Design:** White or light grey background. No brand colour background
- **Content:** Google/Checkatrade/Facebook logos with star ratings and review count. "Fully Insured." "Written Guarantee." "Local to [Location]." All on one line with icons

### Section 4 — Social Proof (Reviews)

Social proof is one of the most powerful conversion drivers. Must be visible without scrolling on desktop.

- **Design:** White background. Near-black heading
- **Review cards:** 3–6 cards in a grid. Each card includes: circular profile photo, reviewer name, 5 gold stars, review text, platform icon, job type + local area
- **Authenticity:** Natural, grounded language. Include specific job types and local area names
- **Aggregate rating:** Overall star rating and total review count above the cards, alongside platform logos
- End with a text link to the full Google reviews page

### Section 5 — Problem Section

Addresses the homeowner's specific fear or pain point. Validates why they are searching and creates urgency to act.

- **Design:** Light grey background. Image of the problem on one side
- **Headline:** Names the homeowner's specific fear directly
- **Body copy:** Short and direct. Maximum two sentences per paragraph
- **Warning signs:** Bulleted list of 5–6 specific warning signs relevant to the service
- **End:** CTA button in action colour

### Section 6 — USP Section

The key USP gets its own dedicated section — not a bullet point. This is what sets the business apart.

- **Design:** White background. Strong visual related to the USP on one side
- **Headline:** Explains the benefit to the homeowner — not the feature
- **Body copy:** Three sentences max. Plain English. No jargon
- **End:** CTA button

### Section 7 — Services Grid

Presents the range of services in a clean, scannable format.

- **Design:** Light grey background. Four cards in a grid
- **Each card:** Real British photo of that service type, benefit-led headline in near-black, two lines of body copy, outcome statement in the action colour

### Section 8 — Recent Work

Demonstrates real, local results. Builds credibility through specificity.

- **Design:** White background
- **Content:** 2–3 brief job descriptions mentioning specific nearby areas, job type, and outcome. Before/after image placeholders
- **End:** CTA button

### Section 9 — Why Choose [Business Name]

Four specific, verifiable reasons to choose this business over any competitor.

- **Design:** Light grey background. Four points in a grid
- **Each point:** Icon + bold heading + two lines of copy. Must be specific and verifiable — not generic

### Section 10 — Simple 3-Step Process

Reduces anxiety about what happens after the visitor contacts the business.

- **Design:** White background. Three numbered steps with visual indicators
- Step 1: Request a free quote
- Step 2: Free survey or consultation
- Step 3: Fixed price agreed, work completed to guarantee standard
- **End:** CTA button

### Section 11 — Local Area Coverage

Reinforces local relevance for both Google Quality Score and user trust.

- **Design:** Light grey background
- **Content:** Short paragraph naming all target areas. Must feel warm and local — not a keyword dump. Mention the primary location and surrounding towns by name

### Section 12 — Urgency Section

Creates a genuine reason to act now rather than later.

- **Design:** White background
- **Content:** Short, honest urgency message — limited availability, seasonal demand, or a genuine time-sensitive offer. No fake countdown timers. No fabricated scarcity
- **End:** Strong CTA button

### Section 13 — Guarantee Section

The guarantee must be prominent and specific. "All work guaranteed" is not acceptable.

- **Design:** Light grey background. Prominent shield badge graphic
- **Content:** State exactly what is covered, for how long, and what the customer does if something goes wrong
- **Accreditation badges:** Logos for relevant trade organisations, "Fully Insured", certifications

### Section 14 — FAQ Section

Addresses common objections and adds keyword-rich content for Google.

- Questions must be specifically relevant to the keyword the page is targeting — not generic
- Short, direct answers
- Group questions logically
- Must be marked up with FAQPage schema

### Section 15 — Final CTA Section

The last opportunity to convert a visitor who has read the entire page.

- **Design:** Primary brand colour as the full background — one of the only places the brand colour is used as a full background
- **Content:** Strong closing headline in white. Phone number in very large white text. The short form repeated with a white card. "No obligation" reassurance line beneath the submit button

### Section 16 — Footer

- **Design:** Primary brand colour or near-black background
- **Content:** Business name, phone number, email, services list, areas covered, Privacy Policy, Terms, copyright. Clean and simple

### Mobile Sticky Bar

Fixed to the bottom of the screen on mobile devices only. This is the most important conversion element on mobile.

- Two full-width buttons side by side: "Call Now" (tap-to-call, action colour) and "Free Quote" (scrolls to form, white with dark text)
- Must be visible at all times on mobile. Must not be hidden or obscured by other elements

## Part 3 — CRO Principles: Rules That Cannot Be Broken

These rules apply to every landing page without exception. They are not suggestions.

### 3.1 One Page, One Goal

The only actions on this page are: call the phone number, or submit the form. No navigation menu. No external links. No distractions. Every element exists to push the visitor towards one of those two actions. If an element does not serve that goal, remove it.

### 3.2 The Phone Number Appears Five Times

At minimum: sticky header, hero section, mid-page CTA, final CTA section, and footer. On mobile, every instance must be a tap-to-call link using `href="tel:[number]"`. Never display a phone number as plain text only.

### 3.3 Every Section Ends With a Micro-CTA

The reader must never be more than one scroll away from a way to contact the business. Every section ends with a phone number, a "Get a Free Quote" button, or both. Non-negotiable.

### 3.4 Objection Handling Is Woven Throughout

Do not isolate objection handling in a single FAQ at the bottom. Address objections where they naturally arise:

- **Price anxiety:** Addressed in the hero (e.g. "Free no-obligation quote")
- **Trust:** Addressed in the social proof section
- **Disruption and inconvenience:** Addressed in the process section
- **Reliability:** Addressed in the why-choose-us section

### 3.5 Benefits Over Features

Every feature must have a corresponding benefit statement. Do not list what the service is — explain what it does for the homeowner. "20 years of experience" is a feature. "You get a roofer who has seen every problem and knows exactly how to fix it" is a benefit.

### 3.6 Copy Rules

- British English throughout
- Short sentences — maximum two sentences per paragraph
- No passive voice
- Never use: "we are passionate about", "high quality", "second to none", "bespoke", "competitive prices", "we pride ourselves", "look no further"
- Every headline must state an outcome or a specific benefit
- Use real numbers: specific timeframes, review counts, years of experience, guarantee length
- Sound like a trusted local tradesman, not a marketing agency

## Part 4 — Form Design & Friction Reduction

The lead capture form is the primary conversion mechanism. It must be frictionless.

- **Minimal fields:** Name, Phone Number, Postcode, and a single dropdown for service type. No long message boxes
- **Clear CTA button:** Visually distinct, action colour. Copy must be specific (e.g. "Book My Free Survey") — never use "Submit"
- **Privacy assurance:** Short message near the form (e.g. "Your information is safe with us")
- **Mobile optimisation:** Fully responsive, appropriately sized fields and buttons, easy to tap

## Part 5 — SEO & Technical On-Page Optimisation

Google's crawlers evaluate the technical quality of the page as part of their quality assessment.

### 5.1 Meta Title

Include the primary target keyword and location. Under 60 characters. Example: "Emergency Roof Repair Manchester | Free Quote Today"

### 5.2 Meta Description

Include the primary keyword and a clear call to action. Under 160 characters.

### 5.3 Header Tag Structure

- Exactly one H1 tag containing the primary keyword
- H2 tags for major sections, including secondary keywords where natural
- H3 tags for subsections
- Logical hierarchy throughout

### 5.4 Image Optimisation

- Every image has descriptive alt text including relevant keywords
- File names are descriptive (e.g. `emergency-roof-repair-manchester.webp`, not `IMG_1234.jpg`)
- All images compressed. WebP format preferred

### 5.5 Schema Markup

Implement on every page:

- **LocalBusiness schema:** Business name, address, phone number, service area, description
- **FAQPage schema:** Applied to the FAQ section
- **AggregateRating schema:** If testimonials or star ratings are displayed

### 5.6 URL Structure

Clean, descriptive, includes the primary keyword. Example: `yourdomain.co.uk/emergency-roof-repair-manchester`

## Part 6 — PPC Indexation & SEO Best Practices

### 6.1 Do Not Index PPC Landing Pages

Apply `<meta name="robots" content="noindex">` to all PPC-exclusive landing pages. Reasons:

- **Misaligned search intent:** Organic searchers expect information, not sales-heavy pages
- **Skewed analytics:** Organic traffic inflates paid campaign numbers
- **Thin content risk:** Minimal copy and no navigation can harm domain quality evaluation
- **Keyword cannibalisation:** PPC pages competing with organic pages for the same keywords

### 6.2 Use Subdomains, Not Separate Domains

Host landing pages on a subdomain of the client's primary website (e.g. `get.clientdomain.com`) via DNS CNAME records.

- Maintains brand consistency and user trust
- Google Ads Quality Score benefits from the root domain's historical trust
- Avoids Google's "Double Serving" policy violations

### 6.3 Canonical Tags

If the PPC page is similar to an existing organic page, add a `rel="canonical"` tag pointing to the main website's organic page as a backup signal.

## Part 7 — Google Quality Score: Landing Page Experience

Quality Score is determined by Expected Clickthrough Rate, Ad Relevance, and Landing Page Experience. The landing page directly controls the third component.

### 7.1 Relevance of Content

Page content must be directly relevant to the keyword being targeted. Write content that genuinely answers the user's need — do not pad with irrelevant text.

### 7.2 Transparency & Trustworthiness

Google explicitly evaluates whether a page is transparent about who is behind it. Include: business name, contact details, clear service description, and Privacy Policy link.

### 7.3 Mobile-Friendliness

Majority of Google Ads traffic comes from mobile. Large tappable buttons, readable text without zooming, clean layout on small screens. Verify with Google's Mobile-Friendly Test.

### 7.4 Page Load Speed

Target under 2 seconds on a 4G mobile connection. Compress all images. Avoid heavy JavaScript. Verify with Google PageSpeed Insights before going live.

---

## Part 8 — Technical Requirements

### 8.1 Performance

- Page loads in under 2 seconds on 4G mobile
- All images compressed
- No heavy JavaScript frameworks or render-blocking resources
- Vanilla HTML and CSS where possible

### 8.2 Responsiveness

Tested and flawless at three breakpoints:

- **375px** — mobile
- **768px** — tablet
- **1280px** — desktop

Check both portrait and landscape on mobile. Verify mobile sticky bar is visible and functional.

### 8.3 Click-to-Call

All phone numbers use `href="tel:[number]"` links. Never display a phone number as plain text only.

### 8.4 Conversion Tracking

Before going live:

- Google Ads conversion tag installed and tracking form submissions and phone call clicks
- Local tracking phone number connected to call tracking
- Thank-you page or confirmation message set up to fire after form submission

### 8.5 Phone Number Format

The number displayed must be the local area code tracking number assigned to this specific campaign. This ensures familiarity for the visitor and accurate campaign tracking.
